For team shoots, the first practical question is: studio or on-site?

## When studio wins

- **Small teams (3–8 people)** — easier to schedule a 90-minute studio block than to commandeer a meeting room
- **Mixed schedules** — people pop in and out across an afternoon
- **You want maximum consistency** — same backdrop, same light, same room
- **Your office is too dark, too small, or too distracting**

## When on-location wins

- **Teams of 10+** — getting everyone to a studio is a logistics nightmare
- **Limited time windows** — a 3-hour shoot at your office can do 25 people
- **You want environmental shots** — desks, signage, brand context
- **Hybrid teams** — easier to align with whoever's in the office that day

## The hybrid approach

Our most popular setup for larger companies: execs in studio (more time, more polish) and the rest of the team on-site at the office (faster, cheaper). We use the same lighting setup in both — the look stays consistent across the About page.

## What we set up on-location

A meeting room is plenty. We bring:

- Two studio strobes with softboxes
- A 3m roll of seamless backdrop (white, grey, or your brand colour)
- Tethered laptop so people can see their selects
- Small posing platform for height consistency

You provide: a power outlet and a 4×3m clear floor area.
