The honest answer: anywhere from R500 to R5,000 per person, depending on what you're actually buying.

## What's at the bottom end

Mall studios and budget chains charge R500–R900 for a portrait. You get one or two retouched files, harsh lighting, and a generic backdrop. Fine for a passport. Not fine for the leadership page of a serious business.

## What's in the middle

Single-session corporate work in Cape Town typically runs **R1,500–R3,000** per person. At this level you should expect:

- A proper studio with controlled lighting
- A pre-shoot conversation about wardrobe and direction
- Tethered shooting (you see images on a big screen as we shoot)
- Skilled retouching that doesn't make you look plastic
- Files in colour and B&W, web and print sizes
- 48-hour turnaround

This is where our **Essential (R1,950)** and **Signature (R2,750)** sessions sit.

## Team and on-location work

Per-head pricing drops with volume. Our team rate is **R750 per person in studio**. On-site at your office across Cape Town it's **R4,500 setup + R750 per head**.

## What pushes price up

- Major retouching (composite, complex backgrounds)
- Same-day delivery
- Multiple wardrobe changes
- Video/BTS content from the same session
- Photography rights for advertising use

## What you're really paying for

Lighting, direction, retouching judgement, and turnaround speed. Anyone with a camera can take a photo. Headshot photography is the small set of decisions that make the difference between *technically a portrait* and *a portrait you're proud to put on LinkedIn*.
